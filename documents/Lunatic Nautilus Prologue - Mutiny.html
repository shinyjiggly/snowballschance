THE OPENING BATTLE

The game begins immediately with a battle. Dolph, Jeremiah, Cleaver, and seven nameless, generic pirates fight against 5 Natsiya Soldiers on a ship deck. This battle is basically impossible to lose unless you try, and serves primarily as an action-packed opening and to introduce the 10-person battle system.

AFTER THE FIGHT

Upon victory, the scene cuts to a field view of the ship, with the pirates standing before the defeated soldiers.

Dolph: Right, men! Time for payday! Take everything that isn't nailed down, and then some!

Pirates: Aye aye!

The seven generic pirates disappear below deck, and Cleaver turns to Dolph.

Cleaver: I gotta hand it to ye… These Imperial ships turn quite a profit.

Dolph: Reconsidering your position, are you?

Cleaver: Hardly! Yer a fool, Dolph. Merchant ships are far more common, and they put up less of a fight. If ye weren't so damn soft…!

Jeremiah raises his fists.

Jeremiah: Watch your tongue, ribbit! The Captain is your Captain as well!

Dolph: Calm down, Jeremiah. Regardless of your thoughts on our new targets, Cleaver, surely even you have to admit… this feels like old times. The Twin Terrors, remember?

Cleaver: Ha… Maybe so. Maybe so.

A pirate comes up from below deck.

Pirate: Cap’n! Come down, quick!

Dolph: Eh? What is it?

The pirate leads Dolph below deck. Jeremiah and Cleaver follow.

BELOW THE WARSHIP DECK

There, the other pirates stand around a glass case with a glowing deep blue sphere inside. No other objects can be seen.

Cleaver: What?! Is this all they had?!

Pirate: It seems so. We've cleared out their rations and supplies - around a week’s worth - and there was a few thousand gold, but…

Dolph: Stand aside.

The others do so, allowing Dolph to approach the case.

Dolph: Hm… If the Empire devoted an entire ship to this thing, it must be worth something.

Dolph draws his axe and swings it horizontally, shattering the glass case. He picks up the strange orb and places it in his coat pocket.

Dolph: Right! We’ll examine it more closely when we get back to the cove. Now - who’s ready for some grog?

The pirates, save Cleaver, cheer, and the crew exits the hold. Cleaver is the last to leave, and lingers to cast one more glance at the hold… The LUNATIC NAUTILUS title card appears, followed by PROLOGUE: MUTINY. Fade to black.

CONTROLLING CLEAVER

Fade into the pirate ship’s deck, night. All is quiet, until Cleaver enters from below deck.

Cleaver: Can't sleep… Need to breathe in the air o’ the sea.

The player gains control of Cleaver. If they try to go back below deck…

Cleaver: Not yet… I'll stay out here a bit longer.

If they check the steering wheel…

Cleaver: One day, I'll be the one steering.

If they climb to the crow’s nest, Cleaver spots something.

Cleaver: Eh? Is that…

Cut to a small dinghy crossing the sea. Cut back.

Cleaver: Heh… What Dolph don't know won't hurt 'im.

Cut to the dinghy, now closer. A mysterious man stands at the fore, peering out across the sea.

???: ...Damn, it's coming closer.

Cut to the pirate ship, now beside the dinghy with its plank ready for boarding. Cleaver boards the dinghy, sword drawn. The mystery man draws a rapier.

???: Just one of you?

Cleaver: I'll be plenty!

THE MYSTERY MAN

A battle with the mystery man ensues, with the player controlling Cleaver. Though it's harder than the intro battle due to being one-on-one, Cleaver’s high stats and Umbral Magic make it fairly easy. The mystery man fights with a variety of magic, as well as pistol and rapier attacks. The battle does not end with a victory screen, but with Dolph interrupting - this also occurs if Cleaver drops to critical HP, but you'll miss out on an item later.

Dolph: CLEAVER! That's ENOUGH!

DOLPH’S INTERRUPTION

The scene cuts back to the field, with Cleaver and the mystery man standing at a distance, both with weapons drawn. The camera pans to the pirate ship, where Dolph, Jeremiah, and the other 7 pirates stand.

Dolph: Stand down. You know this isn't how we do things now.

Cleaver: Dolph, you damn coward! Yer just gonna let this man an’ his booty go? Because, what, yer scared o’ Hell?

Dolph: No, you idiot!

Cleaver: Then why?!

Dolph: Because if we only raid Imperial ships, we become folk heroes! And then the merchants and adventurers just GIVE us their booty!

Cleaver: That's a lie! And yer--

Jeremiah: Shut up, ribbit! Don't talk back to the Captain!

Dolph: You get back on this ship, and I won't have you walk the plank for this. How's that sound?

Everyone pauses for a moment. Finally, Cleaver sighs and sheathes his sword.

Cleaver: Fine. But just ye wait, Dolph… One day, ye'll slip up, and the Empire will have ye hung fer it.

Dolph turns away.

Dolph: Men! Throw him in the brig!

Pirates: Aye aye!

A pair of pirates grab Cleaver from either side and pull him back onto the ship.

???: Will that be all, then?

Dolph: ...Never speak of this to anyone. You may go.

Dolph walks back below deck, the other pirates following.

THE BRIG

Fade to the brig, with Cleaver behind bars and Dolph standing outside.

Cleaver: You dirty dog…

Dolph: Enough with the insults, Cleaver. Why would you do that? Attack that ship, alone, without permission?

Cleaver: Because there was no reason not to! I nearly had that ponce beaten, and if ye had fought with me and not against me it would have been effortless to kill him!

Dolph: And for what? The few supplies of a wandering adventurer?! Don't try to ascribe some goal to your bloodthirstiness!

Cleaver: And don't try to ascribe one to your phony virtue!

The two stare each other down for a moment.

Dolph: ...What happened to you, Cleaver? To us? We used to fight side by side, the Twin Terrors!

Cleaver: And then you decided to grow a conscience, and focus on the Empire.

Dolph shakes his head.

Dolph: Maybe you're right… But so what if it is out of altruism? Is it really so bad, that we focus our efforts on… doing some good in the world? And even without that, our loot has been better than ever--

Cleaver: Oh, like that little trinket from that last ship?

Dolph pulls the orb from his coat.

Dolph: I'm telling you… if the Natsiyans had that many men defending this thing, it must be worth something. A magical artifact of some sort, or a crown jewel.

Cleaver: Bah… It's not about the loot anyway. It's about our reputation.

Dolph: Are you really so attached to that idea? Of being the terror of the seas?

Cleaver: …

Dolph sighs, then leaves the brig.

Cleaver: ...It's the next best thing to being one of the Twin Terrors.

Fade out.

THE COVE

Fade back into the ship docking inside a cove, with yet more generic pirates waiting. Dolph leads his men out, and they lead Cleaver, his arms tied behind his back. One of the pirates in the cove speaks up.

Pirate: Captain, what happened to Cleaver?

Dolph: He acted against orders - raided a small dinghy piloted [note: look up proper word for this] by some no-name adventurer. We’ll untie him at the crack of dawn tomorrow. Tonight - we drink!

The pirates cheer, and the pirates, save Cleaver, head deeper into the cove. Cleaver looks down as the scene fades out.

WE’RE RAIDING MOLNYA

Fade into the same dock, the next day - Cleaver now asleep. Dolph enters.

Dolph: Oy!

Cleaver doesn't respond, so Dolph approaches and prods him. Cleaver leaps up in comical surprise.

Cleaver: Arr, Imperial bastard! I'll gut ye like a…!

Cleaver pauses and looks over at Dolph.

Cleaver: Ah, it's just you.

Dolph: That it is. We've decided - we're raiding Molnya.

Cleaver: Molnya?! The Imperial port town?!

Dolph: I thought you wanted more action?

Cleaver: ...Aye, I suppose so. Very well, then - I won't be much good with my arms bound. Care to deal with that?

Dolph unties Cleaver.

Dolph: Right, then. I'll gather the men, and then we shall set sail!

Fade out.

CALM BEFORE THE SCORN (please god come up with something better than this)

Fade into the ship, on the high seas. Dolph and Cleaver stand together at the bow, with the other pirates, including Jeremiah, busily operating the ship.

Dolph: We're nearly there. Molnya awaits… just over the horizon.

Cleaver: Hmph. Let's hope things work out.

A voice comes from the crow's nest.

Pirate: Land ho! It's in our sights!

Dolph: Hm… We'd best get prepared. Cleaver, you take the men below deck and get them equipped. I'll make sure we get to our destination.

Cleaver: ...Aye aye. Men! Let's go!

Cleaver and the other pirates disappear below deck. Jeremiah remains behind, however, along with Dolph.

Dolph: ...Shouldn't you be getting equipped?

Jeremiah: I use my bare hands, ribbit. Besides, I would never leave you alone up here.

Dolph: ...Why are you so attached to me, anyway?

Jeremiah: I owe you a life debt. You saved my life, ribbit, and so it is my duty to defend yours.

Dolph: Heh… Guess you frog folks take that sort of thing seriously. Hell, you've hardly left my side since I got you off that slaver ship.

Jeremiah: Well… that part isn't the life debt, ribbit.

Dolph: Eh? What's that supposed to mean?

Jeremiah: There is a kindness in you, ribbit. You may not show it… but yours is a heart I am glad to give myself to.

Dolph: Ah, you flatter me. In any case… I'm glad to have you around, Jeremiah.

Jeremiah: I'm glad you feel that about me, ribbit.

Cleaver, and the other pirates, emerge from below deck.

Cleaver: Everything's in order, Captain.

Dolph: Thank you, Cleaver. Just in time, too.

The camera pans to the front of the ship, where Molnya comes into view. People standing in the area jump in surprise and run off.

Dolph: So much for the element of surprise, eh?

Cleaver draws his sword and grins.

Cleaver: We won't​ need it.

The ship finally reaches a point where the town is alongside it, and Cleaver leaps out, along with two pirates.

Dolph: Right. Jeremiah, you three, you're with me!

Dolph leaps out of the ship, Jeremiah and three generic pirates following.

THE RAID

This leads to a fairly short “dungeon” which is largely a series of setpieces. The first occurs only a short while down the street, when Dolph ducks around a corner to see Natsiyan soldiers marching ahead.

Dolph: Damn… Don't think we can stick to the main roads.

Jeremiah: How about there, ribbit?

Jeremiah points to a nearby alleyway.

Dolph: Perfect.

The party enters the alleyway, and soon stumbles upon two soldiers.

Soldier A: You know, I hear there's some new gang springing up in [ancient ruined empire]

Soldier B: Bah… that place is anarchy. I don't see why we should--

Soldier B notices the pirates.

Soldier B: Hey! Aren't you those pirates?

Dolph grins and draws his axe, and…

FIGHTING THEM HEAD-ON

The battle begins. It's quite easy.

PASSAGE

From here on groups of one to three soldiers appear as encounters. Later, the party is intercepted by seven Natsiyan soldiers.

Soldier: Stop right there, pirate scum!

Dolph: Damn…! Outnumbered!

???: Allow me to help.

A chunk of ice drops into one of the soldiers, killing him. The wanderer Cleaver attacked before enters from behind the party.

Dolph: You…?! What are you doing here, wanderer?

???: You saved me. I'm merely repaying the kindness. Besides, an enemy of the Empire is a friend of mine.

The wanderer gets into a battle stance, the five pirates following.

???: Oh… And call me Daraway.

DARAWAY SHOWS OFF

A battle ensues. Though it's technically six on six, the soldiers are underpowered compared to the party and will go down easily, allowing the player plenty of experimentation with Daraway’s abilities.

MUTINY

Afterwards, Daraway turns to the party.

Daraway: I saw your rude friend on the way here. He's got quite a few pirates with him… and I heard them talk about going after Dolph. That's you, correct?

Dolph: What?! But that's…

Cleaver: Mutiny.

The camera pans to show Cleaver, with a small army of pirates at his back.

Dolph: Cleaver! What is the meaning of this?!

Cleaver: Yer a damn coward, Dolph. An’ yer men have had enough.

The three pirates accompanying the party walk to Cleaver’s side.

Dolph: This is preposterous! When did you even--!

Cleaver: Oh, I've discussed it here and there… when you were out of earshot. But it was below deck, before we landed, when I gave them the final command.

Dolph draws his axe.

Dolph: You bastards! You'll regret standing against--!

Daraway gestures, and a wall of of earth bursts out of the ground between the two groups.

Dolph: Daraway!

Daraway: Sorry, but with the number of men he's got I can't imagine that would have gone well. Let's get out of here, shall we?

Dolph: ...Right. I assume you have a ship?

Daraway: Of course. Come!

Daraway leads the party off, and…

THE DUNGEON CONTINUES.

From here on, small groups of 2-3 pirates will appear in addition to the soldiers, though the two enemy types never appear at the same time. Eventually, Cleaver and his army finally catch up to the party in a chokepoint between the side of a building and the sea.

Cleaver: No more running, friend!

Dolph draws his axe.

Dolph: Jeremiah! Daraway! Go bring the ship around! I'll handle these dogs.

Jeremiah: Captain! Are you sure, ribbit?!

Daraway: This is between them. Come.

Daraway and Jeremiah escape.

Dolph: Enough games, Cleaver! You and me!

Cleaver laughs.

Cleaver: And why would I do that, when all these men swear allegiance to me?

Dolph: You wouldn't…!

Cleaver: Go get 'im!

The pirates advance, and…

BATTLE AGAINST CLEAVER

A battle ensues. Cleaver is technically present, but he is behind 4 pirates, and cannot be attacked until all of them are defeated. Making this more difficult is the fact that, every 2 turns, if at least one of the pirates has been defeated, an additional pirate will join the fray. This never stops happening, and Cleaver is attacking with dark magic all the while. Whatever the player tries, it will be all they can do to stay alive in this 5-on-1 battle. Thankfully, after around 20 turns…

Jeremiah: Captain!

RESCUE

Cut back to the field, where Daraway’s ship has arrived.

Dolph: About damn time you showed up!

Jeremiah leaps down and grabs Dolph before jumping back onboard with him.

Daraway: Alright! Let's move!

The ship begins to sail away, and Cleaver rushes to the edge of the town’s platform.

Cleaver: Ye’d best run, yellowbelly! If ye ever show her face here again, I'll cut it off!

Panning back to the ship, Dolph seems regretful as he and his party speed away and the screen fades out. The chapter ends.
